# A process?

* Organizations want a well-defined, well-understood, repeatable software development process.

* Why?
    * Find and repeat good practices
    * Management
        * know what to do next
        * know when we're done with current task
        * know if we're late
        * estimate time to completion, costs
    * New team members know what to do

> The creation and tranlation of human needs into requirements, requirements into design, design into code, testing, installing, etc
---
# Software Process

* All processes have some form of:
    * Requirements Elicitation
    * Requirements Specification
    * Design
    * Development(design, implemented, verified)
    * Validation
    * Evolution(AKA, maintence)

* The form/ iteration/ timing/ etc. of these activities defines different process families and methodologies

---
# Software Process Models

* Brodaly speaking, process models and thus methodologies fall on a continuum

* ## Agile method
    * scrum, extreme programming
    * rapid iteration
    * moving through software version continuously
* ## Plan-Driven
    * focus on specific phase intensively

---

# Choosing a Software Process

* ## Organizational factors
    * Is the team co-located or global?
    * How is the team structured(Are thre PMs? Small teams? Large Team?)

* ## Technology factors
    * How does the team like to work? Whiteboard or online tracking?
    * Do you have access to software to manage communication effectively?

* ## Domain/Sector factors
    * how quick is the turnaround on a products?
    * Are there constant updates?

* ## Regulatory factors
    * Are you building something that requires government or other third-party oversight?
    * What docs / artifiacts will you have to provide?


* ### Agile Methodologies
    * Scrum
    * Extreme Programming(XP)
    * Kanban
* ### Plan-Driven Methodologies
    * Rational Unified Process(RUP)
    * Personal Software Process(PSP)
    * Team Software Process(TSP)
